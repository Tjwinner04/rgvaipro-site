The Cost of Voicemail

Here's what happens when calls go to voicemail:

Voicemail is a "Closed" Sign

Voicemail tells customers: "We're not available."

A voice agent tells customers: "You're next."

People don't call to leave messages. They call to get help now. If they hit voicemail, they call the next business.

The "Leak in the Bucket" Math

10 missed calls per week

→ Only 3 are real customers (rest are spam/shoppers)

→ You close 1 job out of those 3

→ Average job: $250–$800

$250–$800/week leaking = $1,000–$3,200/month

A voice agent stops the leak by catching real callers and handing you clean, ready-to-close leads.

It's Not Answering Calls. It's Selling for You.

Voicemail captures nothing reliably.

A voice agent captures the 5 things that make you money:

  • Name + call-back number (confirmed)
  • What they need (service)
  • Where they are (zip/address)
  • When they want it (today vs later)
  • Urgency (emergency vs normal)

"New lead: Ana, 956-xxx. Water heater leak. Weslaco. Wants today. Okay with ETA 2–4pm."

That's a lead you can close in 30 seconds.

It Protects Your Reputation

Owners don't feel the pain until they see it:

  • Missed calls → bad reviews ("never answered")
  • Lost jobs → slow weeks
  • Constant interruptions → stress

The agent becomes your 'front desk' without hiring someone full-time.
